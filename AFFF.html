AFFF Information

The AFFF (Aqueous Film-Forming Foam) mass tort litigation represents one of the most significant legal battles in recent history. This litigation centers around allegations that AFFF, a specialized firefighting foam used to suppress flammable liquid fires, contains toxic PFAS "forever chemicals" that cause cancer and other serious health conditions. These chemicals don't break down naturally and can remain in human tissues for years, leading to devastating health consequences for those exposed.
Current Litigation Status
As of March 2025, over 8,400 AFFF lawsuits have been consolidated into multidistrict litigation (MDL) in the U.S. District Court for the District of South Carolina. The litigation continues to gain momentum with 338 new cases added in February 2025 alone. Unlike a class action where plaintiffs are treated as one group, the MDL structure allows each case to remain individual with separate outcomes based on unique circumstances.
The first bellwether trial addressing kidney and testicular cancer claims is scheduled for October 6, 2025. This will be a crucial test case that may influence future settlements. Meanwhile, the court has expanded its review to include other health risks such as liver cancer and thyroid cancer.
Scientific Evidence and Health Concerns
Studies have consistently linked PFAS chemicals in AFFF to numerous serious health conditions. The scientific evidence shows these chemicals accumulate in the body over time, affecting major organs and disrupting normal cellular function.
Plaintiffs in AFFF lawsuits allege that exposure to these chemicals has caused various forms of cancer and other health problems, including:
Kidney cancer
Testicular cancer
Bladder cancer
Prostate cancer
Thyroid cancer and disease
Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
Liver cancer
Pancreatic cancer
Breast cancer
Leukemia
Multiple myeloma
Ulcerative colitis
Legal Basis for Claims:
The litigation centers on allegations that manufacturers knew about the dangers of PFAS in AFFF but failed to warn users or provide safer alternatives. Internal company documents reveal that manufacturers such as 3M and DuPont were aware of the toxic nature of PFAS as early as the 1970s, yet continued to produce and market AFFF.
Major defendants in these lawsuits include 3M, DuPont, Chemours, Tyco Fire Products, Chemguard, Kidde-Fenwal, Honeywell International, Archroma, BASF Corporation, and Dynax Corporation. These companies face claims of negligence, gross negligence, failure to warn, defective design, and fraudulent concealment.
Compensation for Victims
Manufacturers have already agreed to pay billions in settlements, including a $730 million settlement from Carrier Global and a $750 million settlement from Tyco Fire Products.
These settlements aim to compensate victims for:
Medical expenses and treatments
Medical expenses and treatments
Lost wages and diminished earning capacity
Pain and suffering
Emotional distress
Ongoing medical monitoring needs
Recent settlements suggest that significant compensation may be available for those who qualify. The growing number of verdicts against AFFF manufacturers demonstrates the courts' recognition of the serious nature of these cases.
Who Qualifies for an AFFF Lawsuit?
Those who may qualify to file an AFFF lawsuit include:
Firefighters exposed to AFFF during training or emergency response
Military personnel who used AFFF at military bases
Airport workers exposed to firefighting foam
Industrial workers who handled AFFF
Residents living near facilities where AFFF was regularly used
To qualify, individuals typically must have been diagnosed with one of the associated cancers or health conditions after exposure to AFFF-containing products.
Time is Limited to File Your Claim
Each state has its own statute of limitations that dictates how long you have to file an AFFF lawsuit, typically ranging from 1 to 3 years from diagnosis or discovery of harm. The "discovery rule" may extend this timeline in some cases, but it's crucial to act quickly to protect your legal rights.
